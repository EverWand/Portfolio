  • Tim Sort vs. Gnome Sort Analysis

    In this program, I pit against two different sorting Algorithms of Sorting Algorithms, curious as to how they would compare when handling different sized lists to sort through. I have constructed both a Gnome Sort and a Tim Sort algorithm that takes in randomly generated lists of values. I ran tests to see just how fast each sorting algorithm was when processing each list. With Tim Sort incorporating both the Insertion Sort and Merge Sort algorithms, I was able to gather more insight than just the two algorithms I began my focus on. In an API document detailing the program and test results, I graphed both the Worst-Case time complexities for the two algorithms to see how they compared with the results I had gathered.

    Establish collaboration, mentorship and professional leadership skills by working with other disciplines to deliver highly polished and completed projects.

  • The Cursed Men

    The Cursed Men was one of the first larger teams I’ve been on, consisting of around fifteen other developers who put their efforts towards the project, all with their own wealth of knowledge and hard work when it came to their specific principles and roles on the team. I imagine myself no different, stepping up with my experience leading small teams prior to the project, I became the person in charge of implementing the UI and the lead programmer. We would have two meetings a week where we discussed our stand-ups and how to tackle problems. One of these meetings would be to make a build of where the project’s progress was so we can analyze where we were at and what possibly needed to be fixed or changed. This build-review provided an outlet to not only be critical about what needed to happen, but served as a way to celebrate our efforts and see the project grow each week. I would often be on stand-by to provide help and ensure we made progress for each week’s build, while also handling my own UI tasks.

    Razor Surfer

    I worked on Razor Surfer with a small, diverse team of six developers, including designers, programmers, artists, and a skilled composer, for UAT’s 2024 Founder’s Game Jam. My primary role was to implement the game’s UI, while also handling various programming tasks and feature adjustments as they arose. Despite the two-day deadline, I committed fully to my responsibilities, staying focused on delivering a polished result for the team and creating a memorable piece of work.
